“Perchlorate on Mars has two opposing aspects, it poses a serious risk to astronauts but can also be a life-saving resource. As such, ClO4 on Mars ought to be considered an SKG that needs to be addressed prior to exploration of the planet by humans. Inhalation of ClO4−-bearing dust particles could be a major concern, but mitigation technologies exist in the mining industry that could be applied on Mars. Perhaps the most efficient and cost-effective mechanism to mitigate the risk of ClO4 toxicity on Mars is by developing biochemical systems that decompose ClO4 into innocuous Cl–and usable O2, based on concentrated extracts of natural enzymes. This way, mitigation of ClO4 toxicity could be coupled to in situ resource utilization.” — Perchlorate on Mars: a chemical hazard and a resource for humans

Perchlorate is therefore making the Martian soil toxic, but could be used to create propellant for instance. Martian soil is toxic, due to relatively high concentrations of perchlorate compounds containing chlorine.
